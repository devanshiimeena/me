WHY READING FEELS<
IMPRESSIVE AGAIN

a few months into ground school, and I've learned almost as much about patience as I have about airspace.

when I started studying for my pilot's theory exams, I expected the hard part to be the material — meteorology, navigation, the physics of flight. it is hard. but the real challenge has been sitting with something I'm not naturally good at yet, without letting that shake my confidence.

"YOU DON'T NEED TO BE FAST. YOU NEED TO BE SURE."

that's something an instructor said early on, and it's stuck with me more than any formula has. it applies just as much to painting or a branding project as it does to a checklist in a cockpit — get it right, not rushed.

I'm still early in this. there will be more posts about the parts I get wrong before I get them right. that feels like the honest way to write about it.
